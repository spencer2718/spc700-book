# Expected SPC debugger trace — Chapter 5

This is what you should see in Mesen2's SPC debugger as you step
through the solution. Use this to verify your own version produces
identical (or equivalent) state.

## Initial state, immediately after IPL upload

```
PC = $0200
A  = $00
X  = ?? (set by the SPC's IPL ROM during init; non-zero)
Y  = $00
SP = ?? (set by the SPC's IPL ROM; typically near $EF)
PSW = $02 (Z flag set, others clear)
```

ARAM at $0500: `00`

The exact X and SP values depend on what the IPL ROM happens to
have done; what matters for this exercise is that PC starts at
$0200 with A == 0.

## After step 1 — `mov a, #$42`

The first instruction loads A with the immediate value $42. The
opcode for `MOV A, #imm` is $E8, followed by the immediate byte.

```
PC = $0202     (advanced by 2 bytes: opcode E8 + operand 42)
A  = $42       *** changed ***
PSW: Z flag now clear (loaded value is non-zero).
```

ARAM at $0500: `00`  (unchanged — we haven't stored yet)

## After step 2 — `mov $0500, a`

The second instruction stores A's value to absolute address $0500.
The opcode for `MOV !abs, A` is $C5, followed by the two address
bytes in little-endian order.

```
PC = $0205     (advanced by 3 bytes: $C5 $00 $05)
A  = $42       (unchanged — store doesn't modify A)
PSW: unchanged (store doesn't affect flags).
```

ARAM at $0500: `42`  *** changed ***

## After step 3 — `bra forever`

BRA is a 2-byte instruction with a signed 8-bit relative offset.
For a self-loop, the offset is -2 ($FE) so PC ends up back at the
BRA itself.

```
PC = $0205     (back to the BRA — we are in the infinite loop)
```

ARAM at $0500: `42`

From this point, stepping just bounces PC at $0205 → $0205. The
SPC sits here forever, which is exactly what we want.
